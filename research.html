Research

Research

I use molecular dynamics simulation and theory to understand different phenomena in physical, chemical, and biological systems.

  1. Polymer Dynamics

    Polymer Translocation

    Polymer translocation is the crossing of a polymer through a biological pore. The fundamental understanding of the process is exciting from many interdisciplinary perspectives, such as statistical mechanics, biophysics, and computer simulations. It has several technological and biological applications like DNA sequencing, virus genome analysis, and drug delivery. The dynamics are influenced by many factors.

    Broadly, two kinds of experimental realization of polymer translocation have occurred:

    1. Pore-driven Translocation

      2. The polymer experiences force due to the voltage difference on two sides of a membrane. It is affected by electrostatic mutation on the pore, different chemical environments like solvents, pH, ions, etc.

    2. End-pulled Translocation

      3. The polymer is pulled by holding at one end with optical/magnetic tweezers. Langevin dynamics simulations are helpful to understand single chain and folded dynamics by calculating tension propagation, monomer waiting time distributions, etc.

  2. Crystallization

    Although challenging in computer time-scales, atomistic simulations of crystal growth provide a detailed mechanistic view of the process. Crystal growth phenomena are important in APIs purification, photophysical properties, energy storage in carbon dioxide and methane clathrate hydrates, to name a few.

  3. Thermodynamics in Small Systems

    Entropy production and the second law of thermodynamics become elusive at small length and time scales. The apparent macroscopic irreversibility hides in the system's fluctuations. Understanding small systems in terms of fluctuations in thermodynamic variables such as work, heat, and entropy is crucial.
